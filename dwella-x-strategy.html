Hashtag Strategy

X hashtags work differently from Instagram. Less is more — 1-3 hashtags maximum per tweet. Overusing hashtags looks spammy on X and actually decreases engagement.

Rules

  • Threads: 0–1 hashtags. Let the content speak. Add one hashtag on the last tweet if any.
  • Single tweets: 1–2 hashtags maximum. Integrate naturally into the sentence when possible.
  • Polls: 1 hashtag max, or none.
  • Never: Stack hashtags at the end like Instagram. Never use more than 3.

Thread Strategy

Threads are X's highest-engagement format — they're the equivalent of Instagram carousels. A well-crafted thread can go viral and drive hundreds of profile visits.

Thread Structure

Tweet #PurposeWhat to Write
1 (Hook)Stop the scrollBold claim, surprising stat, or relatable pain point. End with "🧵 Thread" or "A thread 👇"
2–7 (Body)Deliver valueOne idea per tweet. Keep each under 240 chars. Use line breaks for readability.
Last (CTA)ConvertSoft CTA: "Follow @dwella_ai for more" or "Link in bio for waitlist"

Thread Best Practices

  • Hook is everything. 80% of your effort should go into tweet #1. If the hook doesn't stop the scroll, nobody reads the rest.
  • One idea per tweet. Never cram two points into one tweet.
  • Use line breaks. Dense paragraphs get skipped. White space = readability.
  • Number your points. "1/ ", "2/ " etc. makes threads scannable.
  • End with value, not just a CTA. Your last tweet should give something (a summary, a takeaway) before asking for a follow.
  • Self-reply immediately. Post the entire thread at once (write in drafts, then post). Don't drip-feed over hours.
  • Add an image to tweet #1. Threads with a hook image get 2x more impressions.

1 HOOK Your tenant's HRA claim just got rejected. Reason? Your rent receipt was missing 3 fields. Here's exactly what a valid rent receipt needs in India (save this) 👇
2 A valid rent receipt MUST include: ✅ Landlord's full name ✅ Landlord's PAN (if rent > ₹1,00,000/year) ✅ Complete property address ✅ Rent amount per month ✅ Payment period (from — to dates) ✅ Revenue stamp (for cash payments) ✅ Landlord's signature Miss any of these? Receipt is invalid.
3 The PAN rule most landlords miss: If annual rent exceeds ₹1,00,000 — your PAN is MANDATORY on the receipt. No PAN = tenant's HRA claim gets rejected by their employer. They'll blame you. And they'd be right.
4 Common mistakes that get receipts rejected: ❌ No PAN when rent > ₹1L/year ❌ Missing property address (just "Pune" isn't enough) ❌ No date range mentioned ❌ Handwritten receipts without revenue stamp ❌ Generic format downloaded from random website
5 What format should you use? PDF is best. Include: • Your letterhead or name at top • All 7 fields from tweet 2 • Month-wise breakup if giving for full year • Your signature (digital is accepted) Your CA will thank you.
6 Pro tip for landlords: Don't wait for your tenant to ask. Send the receipt proactively in January (for the previous year). It shows professionalism, avoids last-minute panic, and keeps the relationship healthy. Tenants remember landlords who make their life easy.

Thread How to Calculate Rental Yield on Your Property

Post at: 12:30 PM IST • Format: Thread (8 tweets) • Image: Formula graphic on tweet 3

Thread (Copy-Paste Ready)

1 HOOK Your ₹80 lakh flat in Bangalore is earning you 2.5% rental yield. An FD gives you 7%. You're literally losing money by renting it out. Unless you fix this. Here's how to calculate (and improve) your rental yield 👇
2 What is rental yield? The annual return you earn from rent, expressed as a % of your property's market value. It's the single most important number every landlord should know. Yet most Indian landlords have never calculated it.
3 The Formula: Gross Rental Yield = (Annual Rent ÷ Property Value) × 100 That's it. Simple math that most property owners never do.
4 Let's run a real example: Property value: ₹80,00,000 Monthly rent: ₹25,000 Annual rent: ₹3,00,000 Yield = (3,00,000 ÷ 80,00,000) × 100 Yield = 3.75% Is that good? Let's check 👇
5 Rental yield benchmarks in India: 🔴 Below 2% — Terrible. Sell or restructure. 🟡 2–3% — Below average. Most Indian metros sit here. 🟢 3–4% — Average. Decent but improvable. ⭐ 4–5% — Good. You're doing better than most. 🚀 5%+ — Excellent. Commercial or premium locations.
6 But wait — that's GROSS yield. Net yield = (Rent − Expenses) ÷ Property Value × 100 Subtract: • Maintenance charges • Property tax • Insurance • Vacancy months • Repair costs Your net yield is always lower. Often painfully lower.
7 How to actually improve your yield: 1. Price at current market rate (not 2019 rent) 2. Reduce vacancy — respond to enquiries fast 3. Furnish to charge 20–30% premium 4. Track every single payment (don't let money slip) 5. Send timely reminders — reduce late payments Most of these are just discipline + the right tools.
8 CTA We're building @dwella_ai to help landlords stop losing yield to missed payments and disorganized tracking. AI-powered. Built for India. Waitlist is open — link in bio. If this was useful, RT tweet 1 so other landlords see it too 🔄
